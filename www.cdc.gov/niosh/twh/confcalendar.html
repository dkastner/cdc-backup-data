Event: Work and mental health: Capturing natural experiments from longitudinal datasets

Type: Webinar https://attendee.gotowebinar.com/register/6674919954932501506

Sponsor(s): Center for Promotion of Health in the New England Workplace (CPH-NEW)

NIOSH-funded Center of Excellence to Promote a Healthier Workforce

Date: June 26, 2015

Website: http://www.uml.edu/Research/Centers/CPH-NEW/Events.aspx

Description: This webinar is for intervention researchers focused on workplace health, safety, and well-being. Mental health problems among working people are a growing concern to workers, employers, and others. The development and testing of responses to this problem has traditionally relied on intervention research using experimental and related designs, such as cluster-randomised trials. But there is a dearth of such studies due to their great expense, long timelines, and other feasibility challenges. This presentation describes epidemiological methods to emulate experimental designs capturing changes in work and working conditions and associated changes in mental health. Specific examples will include changes at the level of job task, form of employment, and labor market. We argue that capturing natural experiments in this way offers a valuable and efficient complement to experimental studies.

Event: Sedentary Work: Implications and Interventions, NIOSH Total Worker Health Webinar Series

Type: Webinar

Sponsor(s): Office for Total Worker Health

NIOSH Office for Total Worker Health

Date: July 13, 2015

Website: http://www.cdc.gov/niosh/twh/webinar.html

Description: In this free webinar (available for on-demand viewing as well), a panel of experts explores the following questions, and more: What impacts might sedentary work have on injury risk? What impacts does it have on chronic disease? What are some options for addressing physical inactivity at work, and how might the effectiveness of these options vary? What factors influence how likely someone might be to use interventions for sedentary work? And are there key safety and health considerations for ensuring that the interventions themselves are keeping workers protected?
