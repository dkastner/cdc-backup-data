All Tools & Products

Equipment

  • Improved LED Cap Lamp Designs 5/25/2016
    Download the latest NIOSH plans for LED cap lamps that dramatically improve visibility and offer better battery efficiency.
  • NIOSH Saturn Area Light 8/24/2016
    NIOSH developed the Saturn light for roof bolting machines. The design maximizes floor illumination while reducing glare.

Guidelines & Best Practices

  • Best Practices and Bolting Machine Innovations for Roof Screening 2/1/2003
    Injury data show a dramatic reduction in roof skin injuries when screening is used. Five case studies in which roof screen was used are presented along with the costs of materials, impact on bolting advance rates, and potential ergonomic risks.
  • Best Practices for Dust Control in Coal Mining 1/1/2010
    This handbook was developed to identify available engineering controls that can help the industry reduce worker exposure to respirable coal and silica dust.
  • Best Practices for Dust Control in Metal/Nonmetal Mining 5/1/2010
    This handbook was developed to identify available engineering controls that can assist underground and surface metal/nonmetal mining operations in reducing worker exposure to respirable silica dust.
  • Best Practices to Mitigate Injuries and Fatalities from Rock Falls 8/1/2000
    This paper presents an overview of the most significant ground control hazards facing today's mineworkers, as well as best practices to reduce these risks.
  • Design Criteria and Guidelines for Falling Object Protective Structures (FOPS) 2/1/1976
    This study addresses several engineering and economic facets of possible roll-over protective structure (ROPS) and falling object protection structure (FOPS) requirements in the metal-nonmetal mining industry.
  • Guidelines for Instructional Materials on Refuge Chamber Setup, Use, and Maintenance 7/1/2009
    NIOSH has created this document with suggestions for developing manuals and educational materials concerning refuge chambers for miners.
  • Guidelines for Permitting, Construction, and Monitoring of Retention Bulkheads in Underground Coal Mines 6/1/2008
    The guidelines in this report are to be used as a tool to identify areas or conditions that could impact the long-term stability of an underground fluid retention system and to give direction in addressing these situations.
  • Guidelines for the Control and Monitoring of Methane Gas on Continuous Mining Operations 4/1/2010
    This NIOSH publication demonstrates how existing and new engineering controls can be used to reduce face methane levels.
  • Guidelines for the Development of a New Miner Training Curriculum 1/1/2008
    This report is intended to help mine safety trainers better prepare to teach new underground coal miners. It identifies 2 different approaches to instruction, i.e. using a syllabus or using a curriculum, and discusses their pros and cons.
  • Guidelines for the Prediction and Control of Methane Emissions on Longwalls 3/1/2008
    In this report, several practical guidelines are recommended for controlling longwall coalbed methane. All predictions are based on determinations made for the Pittsburgh Coalbed in southwestern Pennsylvania.
  • Pillar and Roof Span Design Guidelines for Underground Stone Mines 5/1/2011
    This document presents the results of research conducted by NIOSH in cooperation with participating underground stone mines to develop generally applicable guidelines for designing stable pillars and roof spans in stone mines.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 1: 1.0 Introduction 4/1/2001
    This report addresses the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment. It is part of a risk-based system safety process encompassing hardware, software, humans, and the operating environment for the equipment's life cycle.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 2: 2.1 System Safety 4/1/2001
    This report is the second in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 3: 2.2 Software Safety 9/1/2001
    This report is the third in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 4: 3.0 Safety File 5/1/2002
    This report is the fourth in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 5: 4.0 Independent Functional Safety Assessment 5/1/2003
    This report is the fifth in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 6: 5.1 System Safety Guidance 8/1/2005
    This report is the sixth in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 8: 6.0 Safety File Guidance 4/1/2006
    This report is the eighth in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Programmable Electronic Mining Systems: Best Practice Recommendations (In Nine Parts): Part 9: 7.0 Independent Functional Safety Assessment Guidance 4/1/2006
    This report is the ninth in a nine-part series of recommendations addressing the functional safety of processor-controlled mining equipment.
  • Roof Screening: Best Practices and Roof Bolting Machines 8/1/2002
    Roof screening has been found to be very effective in controlling the fall of small rocks from the immediate roof surface. Data is presented from two mines that show a dramatic reduction in roof skin injuries when screening is used.

Guides & Handbooks

  • Control Design Principles 9/18/2015
    Selecting the best controls with which an operator interfaces with the equipment is critical to maximizing operator-machine performance
  • Diesel Aerosols and Gases in Underground Mines: Guide to Exposure Assessment and Control 10/1/2011
    A comprehensive guide to establish a program to curtail diesel particulate matter emissions, control pollutants after release in an underground mine environment and reduce exposures using administrative controls.
  • Dust Control Handbook for Industrial Minerals Mining and Processing 1/1/2012
    Mined ore undergoes crushing, grinding, cleaning, drying, and product sizing as it is processed, generating harmful dust. This handbook reviews technologies for lowering dust levels below the permissible or recommended occupational exposure limits.
  • Ergonomics Processes: Implementation Guide and Tools for the Mining Industry 2/1/2009
    This paper describes how three mining companies partnered with the MSD Prevention Team at NIOSH to demonstrate that an ergonomics process could be systematically implemented and effectively integrated with existing safety and health programs.
  • Explosion Pressure Design Criteria for New Seals in U.S. Coal Mines 7/1/2007
    This report provides a sound scientific and engineering justification to recommend a three-tiered explosion pressure design criterion for new seals. The recommendations contained herein apply to new seal design and construction in U.S. coal mines.
  • Guide for the Construction of Driven-Rod Ground Beds 1/1/1978
    This report describes a method for designing and constructing ground beds that has been developed under the sponsorship of the Bureau of Mines. Results of a bed constructed at the Bureau of Mines mine car roadway are presented.
  • Recommendations for Refuge Chamber Operations Training 6/1/2011
    Refuge alternatives are airtight, reinforced shelters that underground coal miners can enter during a mine emergency. This publication provides recommendations for training miners in how to operate a refuge chamber.
  • Seating Design Principles 12/23/2013
    OMSHR has developed a guide for designing seating for undergound mobile mining equipment.
  • Shiftwork: A Guide for Schedule Design 1/1/1994
    The U.S. Bureau of Mines analyzed shiftwork schedule design at mining operations, including various rotation and shift practices. This paper discusses what mining companies can do if they are considering changes in their shiftwork practices.
  • Underground Coal Mine Lighting Handbook (In Two Parts): 1. Background 1/1/1986
    The purpose of the report is to insure an understanding of the numerous complex and interrelated factors that must be considered to design and implement a mine lighting system.
  • Underground Coal Mine Lighting Handbook (In Two Parts): 2. Application 1/1/1986
    This report discusses system design criteria and procedures, data and specifications to aid in selection of suitable mine lighting hardware, and guidelines for system installation and maintenance.
  • Underground Workstation Design Principles 12/23/2013
    This page provides design guidelines to follow for proper workstation design for underground mining equipment.

  • AHSM - Analysis of Horizontal Stress Effects in Mining - 2.4.04 8/20/2013

  • AMSS - Analysis of Multiple Seam Stability - 2.1.02 8/20/2013
    A software program that helps to identify the location and likely severity of multiple seam interactions
  • AQE - Air Quantity Estimator - 1.0.3 3/15/2006
    Software that provides a starting point for estimating the air quantity needed to dilute diesel particulate matter (DPM) in an underground large-opening mine, taking into account various input assumptions.
  • ARBS - Analysis of Roof Bolt Systems - 2.5.02 8/20/2013
    A software program to help select roof bolts for primary support in underground coal mines
  • ARMPS - Analysis of Retreat Mining Pillar Stability - 6.2.02 8/20/2013
    Software for designing pillars for room-and-pillar retreat mining.
  • ARMPS-HWM - Analysis of Retreat Mining Pillar Stability - Highwall Mining - 1.3.02 8/20/2013
    A software program for designing web and barrier pillars for Highwall Mining.
  • CMRR - Coal Mine Roof Rating - 2.1.03 8/20/2013
    Software for estimating the relative inherent strength of coal mine roof.
  • DOSES - Determination of Sound Exposures - 1.1 2/4/2012
    Software to simplify the record-keeping and analysis associated with time-motion studies and worker noise exposures, making it easier to identify and solve noise problems.
  • ErgoMine - 1.0 4/1/2016
    Download the new ErgoMine Android app for conducting ergonomics audits of bagging, maintenance & repair, and haul truck operations in the mining industry.
  • EVADE Software - 2.0 12/1/2016
    EVADE 2.0 has been updated with expanded video and data features to make it a more generalized and powerful tool.
  • Ground Control Software Toolbar - 1.1.02 8/20/2013
    This installable toolbar provides quick access to seven NIOSH ground control software packages.
  • How Can I Test My Hearing Protection? 9/25/2015
    Use the sound player on this page to perform a quick test of whether you are getting at least a minimal 15 decibel (dB) level of protection.
  • MCP - Methane Control and Prediction - 2.0 9/1/2010
    A software program that addresses some of the methane and methane control issues in longwall coal mines in the U.S. and other countries.
  • MFIRE - 3.0.50 12/2/2016
    A software program for modeling fire and contaminant spread.
  • S-Pillar - Software for Stone Mine Pillar Design - 1.02 5/29/2015
    Software for stone mine pillar design.
  • SponCom - Spontaneous Combustion Assessment Software - 2.0 9/1/2011
    Software to aid mining operators, regulatory bodies, and consultants in the assessment of the spontaneous combustion risk of an underground mining operation.
  • STOP - Support Technology Optimization Program - 3.4 4/15/2010
    Software to assist in making engineering decisions regarding the selection and placement strategy of various standing roof support technologies.

  • HLSim - NIOSH Hearing Loss Simulator - 3.0.1215.1 12/5/2010

  • Refuge Chamber Expectations Training - 1.0 10/1/2009

  • When Do You Take Refuge? Decisionmaking During Mine Emergency Escape 8/1/2011
    This training exposes trainees to the types of decisions that they may need to make during a mine emergency escape and stimulate group discussion about when and why to use a refuge alternative.

    The first step to emergency preparedness and maintaining a safe workplace is defining and analyzing hazards.
  • Harry’s Hard Choices: Mine Refuge Chamber Training 3/1/2009
    This paper-and-pencil simulation teaches miners about issues related to self-rescue and escape, including information gathering, knowing one's escapeways, use of SCSRs, the value of multigas detectors, and when to enter a refuge chamber.

  • Man Mountain’s Refuge: Refuge Chamber Training Instructor’s Guide and Trainee’s Problem Book 7/1/2011

  • Nonverbal Communication for Mine Emergencies 11/1/2011
    Nonverbal Communication for Mine Emergencies is a training program that teaches miners a series of nonverbal hand signals to use in the event of an emergency. These hand signals can be used by miners if they are unable to communicate verbally.
  • Radio 101: Operating Two-Way Radios Every Day and in Emergencies 8/1/2011
    Radio 101 is a program to train miners in the use of two-way radio communication in the mines.
