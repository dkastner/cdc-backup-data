Return Home from Work Safely and with Dignity

A family of materials for Spanish-speaking immigrant workers

Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

The National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) has released a series of illustrated communication products for organizations that serve Spanish-speaking immigrant workers. The family of products, entitled Protéjase en el trabajo (Protect yourself at work), includes 4 brochures, 5 short videos, and 2 posters, and is a result of a multi-faceted project that includes a partnership with the Mexican Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and encourages immigrant workers to seek help if they have a question about their workplace safety or health.

  • The NIOSH Protéjase materials, all in Spanish, include print and video materials that present a family of workers and cover a basic orientation to occupational safety and health, common types of hazards, suggestions for how to address safety concerns at work, as well as basic safety recommendations for hotel and construction workers.
  • The Protéjase project team developed these occupational safety and health materials with the three following goals: 1) to help immigrant-serving organizations raise awareness among their clients about occupational safety and health and how to advocate for themselves 2) provide occupational safety and health organizations with outreach tools specific to Spanish-speaking worker populations, and 3) foster partnerships and awareness that will bring organizations which provide high-quality services in both of these areas together.
  • NIOSH partnered with the 50 Mexican Consulates to create support for the Protéjase project and help workers act on the occupational safety and health information they receive through the materials.

  • Although the consulates are trusted venues with an established infrastructure serving to connect immigrant workers with their local resources, the Protéjase materials can be distributed by any organization that serves Spanish-speaking immigrant communities.

The illustrator of the Protéjase materials: Chi-Yun Lau

Chi-Yun Lau is a designer and illustrator, living in Boston. An experienced creative artist who's worked in diverse fields, his work for NIOSH has captured aspects of the immigrant story that inspire action; his illustrations show a rare quality of drawing the viewer in, resonating with their personal experiences, and animating a response in workers that motivates them towards a specific action. His unique style seamlessly blends the overlap of family, work, and safety and health, and provides a visual storyboard that acknowledges what Spanish-speaking immigrant workers may face on the job, what is important to them, and what they can do to return safely to their families at the end of the day.

Spokespersons

Michael Flynn, MA

Michael Flynn, MA

Biography

"NIOSH has been working closely with both the Ventanillas de Salud and the Departamento de Proteccion within the U.S. Mexican Consulate system to find ways to integrate occupational safety and health into their existing activities and infrastructure. The Protejase materials were created as part of NIOSH's mission to conduct research and determine effective ways of delivering occupational safety and health information."

"The focus of this ongoing collaboration between NIOSH and the Mexican government is to develop the internal capacity of these Mexican institutions to promote occupational safety and health among Mexican immigrants while simultaneously developing NIOSH's internal capacity to create effective and sustainable initiatives to better document and reduce occupational health disparities for Mexican immigrants in the U.S."

Michael Flynn, MA - Assistant Coordinator for Priority Populations and Health Disparities, National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health

Pietra Check, MPH

Pietra Check, MPH

"Increasing awareness of workplace hazards, exposures, and basic protection measures is a first step to improving Mexican immigrants' abilities to protect themselves on the job. The Mexican consular network provides institutional support that can help immigrant workers find the resources they need to overcome the many barriers to workplace safety that they may face."

Pietra Check, MPH - Deputy Director of the NIOSH Office of Agriculture Safety and Health and Coordinator of the Agriculture, Forestry & Fishing Program

Amy Filko

Amy Filko

"The Protejase materials use illustrations to represent occupational safety and health concepts, an art form chosen for its emotional impact on the viewer as well as potential for accuracy. Thorough testing of illustrations became necessary to make sure we had overcome our personal tastes and professional habits, and to determine a particular artist who would resonate with the audience."

Amy Filko, BA - Visual Information Specialist in the World Trade Center Health Program, National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health

Nura Sadeghpour

Nura Sadeghpour

"To test the illustrations for impact, we used a standardized protocol following the principles of qualitative research and analysis grounded directly in community participant quotes; the result was a set of materials with illustrations as key to communicating the intended public health messages, adding meaning and understanding, and motivating action."

Nura Sadeghpour, MPH - Health Communication Specialist in the Office of the Director, National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health
