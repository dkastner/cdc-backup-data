Boots on the Ground: CDC’s Disease Detective Conference

Epidemic Intelligence Services (EIS) officers present findings on Ebola, EV D68, MERS and more

Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) will hold its 64th Annual Epidemic Intelligence Service (EIS) conference April 20-23 in Atlanta. The event showcases recent ground-breaking and often times life-saving investigations by CDC disease detectives.

EIS officers will be sharing details of investigations that placed them in the thick of some of the world’s most challenging public health events. Monday through Thursday, CDC’s EIS officers will present their research findings from U.S. and international-based investigations conducted over the past year. CDC Director Tom Frieden, M.D., M.P.H., will open the conference with remarks on Monday morning and attend several sessions throughout the week.

During the past year, nearly every current EIS officer deployed in support of the Ebola response in West Africa. Even so, Ebola is only one of many health threats that CDC disease detectives have investigated. Featured presentations on the conference agenda demonstrate the wide range of public health activities that EIS officers are involved in at CDC.

  • A CDC disease detective helps a healthcare worker learn how to prepare for work in an Ebola treatment unit at a training course held in Anniston, AL.

  • A CDC disease detective investigates an outbreak of tickborne relapsing fever at a high school football camp in Arizona.

  • A CDC disease detective investigates the impact of increasing bike share lanes in New York City.

  • A CDC disease detective investigates chikungunya exposure among volunteers returning from the Dominican Republic.

  • A CDC disease detective helps US hospitals prepare for potential Ebola patients.

  • A CDC disease detective investigates cases of norovirus on overnight tour buses arriving at Yellowstone National Park.

A sample of topics and abstracts that will be presented at this year’s conference

  • Hot Topics
  • Ebola
  • Foodborne Outbreaks
  • Legionnaires'
  • Tuberculosis

Chikungunya and Dengue Virus Infections Among United States Community Volunteers Returning from the Dominican Republic, 2014: In late 2013, chikungunya virus, a mosquito-borne disease, was identified for the first time in the Americas in the Caribbean region.  In August 2014, nearly half of the service volunteers who worked with an organization in the Dominican Republic during the summer months tested positive for Chikungunya virus infection.

Norovirus Syndromic Surveillance Among Commercial Tour Bus Passengers: A Pilot Project – Yellowstone National Park, August 2014: During a pilot project, a CDC disease detective uncovered five potential norovirus clusters on overnight Yellowstone National Park tour buses in August 2014, suggesting a new way to potentially ruin a vacation.

The Role of Internet Meet-up Sites and Mobile Device Applications in Facilitating an Early Syphilis Outbreak – Multnomah County, Oregon, 2014: Are phone apps helping some diseases go viral? EIS officers looked at how technology is changing the way syphilis spreads -- and how it may offer new tools for intervention.

Calls to US Poison Centers Regarding Electronic Cigarettes – United States, September 2010 – October 2014: A concerning new trend: 58% of e-cigarette calls to U.S. poison centers are for concerns about exposures in young children.

Parking Prices and Walking and Bicycling to Work in US Cities: Physical activity is important for health, but less than half of Americans get enough activity through recreation. Active commuting is another way more Americans could be active.  Across several densely populated cities, the number of people walking to work is 3.4% higher for every extra dollar charged for a day’s parking.

Risk of Ectopic Pregnancy Associated With Assisted Reproductive Technology – United State, 2001-2011: Contrary to popular belief, in-vitro fertilization carries about the same risk of ectopic pregnancy -- implantation of an embryo outside the womb -- as normal pregnancy. But ectopic pregnancy risk increases with higher numbers of transferred embryos.

Bicycling in the Boroughs: Trends in Self-reported Bicycling – New York City, 2007-2013: More biking, but who’s biking more? NYC has seen increases in residents biking, but increases might be from increased biking frequency of persons already biking rather than recruitment of new bikers.

Is Presumptive Treatment Presumptuous? The Association Between Presumptive Treatment for Gonorrhea and Patients’ Receipt of Test Results – Maricopa County, Arizona, 2013-2014: When we treat people for gonorrhea before their results are in, are we missing opportunities to make sure their partners can get tested and treated?

Antibody Levels 20 Years After Receipt of Hepatitis A Vaccine: New findings show hepatitis A vaccination protects for at least 20 years.

Increases in Crimean-Congo Hemorrhagic Fever Case Detection – Country of Georgia, 2014: Cases of Crimean Congo Hemorrhagic Fever, a high-priority bioterrorism agent naturally transmitted by ticks, appear to be increasing in the country of Georgia. But is this hemorrhagic fever really on the rise?

Middle East Respiratory Syndrome-Coronavirus in an Extended Family: Risk Factors for Household Transmission – Saudi Arabia, 2014: In spring 2014, CDC disease detectives worked alongside Saudi Arabia Ministry of Health colleagues to investigate an outbreak of MERS in a Saudi extended family and were able to assess household interactions that may be important for spread of MERS.  Since then, MERS cases have continued to be identified in the Middle East.

Severe Respiratory Illness Associated with a Nationwide Outbreak of Enterovirus D68 – United States, 2014:A CDC disease detective investigated the largest reported national outbreak of EVD68 to better understand who was most affected. Most of the patients hospitalized with respiratory disease were children and many had a history of asthma; more than half required intensive care.

Increase in Vibrio Alginolyticus in the United States, 1988-2012: Vibrio alginolyticus infections are increasing, especially on the Gulf coast. Surprisingly, 86% of infections are NOT foodborne -- most are infections of the ear or leg related to swimming or exposing a skin wound to water.

Public Health Response to an Imported Case of Ebola Virus Disease — New York City, 2014: As healthcare workers return from West Africa, close monitoring and rapid response are critical to decrease the risk of Ebola in the U.S. Learn how New York City responded when a returning healthcare worker developed Ebola after working in a West African Ebola treatment unit.

Ebola Virus Disease Outbreak Complicated by Patient’s Efforts to Avoid Diagnosis – Nigeria, 2014: Two contacts of an Ebola patient nearly ignited a huge outbreak in Nigeria. As it was, their actions extended the outbreak by a month and directly endangered 575 people.

Assessing Domestic Readiness for the Treatment of Ebola Virus Disease Patients: Rapid Ebola Preparedness Teams – United States, 2014: Getting U.S. hospitals ready for Ebola was a massive, rapid, meticulous effort: Inside CDC’s Rapid Ebola Preparedness (REP) teams.

Geospatial Analysis of Household Spread of Ebola Virus in a Quarantined Village – Sierra Leone, 2014: In West Africa, Ebola has spread quickly in remote villages. Disease detectives used Geospatial Information Systems (GIS) and computer modeling to investigate how Ebola swept through one village in Sierra Leone, where cases occurred in 27 of 64 households.

Readying the Responders – Infection Control Training for US Clinicians Bound for West Africa: When Ebola cases began to surge in West Africa, it was clear that more doctors were needed to help. Get a look inside CDC's rigorous 3-day training course in protective gear for clinicians volunteering to treat Ebola patients in West Africa.

Rapid Containment of Ebola Using Contact Tracing Following an Imported Case of Ebola Virus Disease – Senegal, 2014: Ebola might well have become a much larger problem in Senegal - but a rapid intervention by Senegal, WHO, CDC and others helped avert a major epidemic in this country.

Pivoting From Preparedness to Response: Containment of the First Outbreak of Ebola Virus Disease in a Previously Unaffected Country – Mali, 2014: Why hasn't Ebola spread to vulnerable nations like Mali? Preparedness and, when Ebola popped up, a rapid response. CDC's Mali team reports.

Outbreak of Salmonella Newport Infections Linked to Cucumbers – United States, 2014: What’s going on with your salad? An outbreak of Salmonella Newport linked to cucumbers was reported on the Delmarva Peninsula. Could this be a sign of recurring outbreak potential?

Multistate Outbreak of Multiple Salmonella Serotype Infections Linked to Sprouted Chia Seed Powder – United States, 2014: More doubts about sprouts. Disease detectives traced 16-state Salmonella outbreak to a superfood: sprouted chia powder.

Toxigenic Virbio Cholerae Serogroup non-O1, non-O139 Infections in the United States, 1984-2014: Certain strains of Vibrio cholerae cause cholera, but even those that do not can cause severe illness. 52 cases of these non-cholera infections have been reported to CDC from 1984 through 2014—most linked to seafood, especially raw oysters from the U.S. Gulf Coast.

Campylobacter Jejuni Infection Associated with Raw Milk Consumption – Utah, 2014: Despite routine testing, raw milk from a Utah dairy sickened 99 people with Campylobacter; 1 died and 10 were hospitalized. A 2-month shutdown failed to stop the outbreak and the dairy's raw milk permit was revoked.

Outbreak of Listeria Monocytogenes Infections Linked to Whole Apples Used in Commercially Produced, Prepackaged Caramel Apples - United States, 2014-2015: New Advanced Molecular Detection technology helped CDC disease detectives solve the first-ever Listeria outbreak in caramel apples, a popular holiday treat.

Rapid Detection of a Winter Outbreak of Legionellosis — New York City, 2014–2015: For rapidly escalating community outbreaks, speed of response can help save lives. New tools are speeding detection of Legionnaires’ outbreaks, such as one in a large NYC housing complex.

Description of Nosocomial Legionella Infections – Los Angeles County, October 2005-August 2014: Current investigation practices for hospital-associated Legionella infection may not be enough to detect and prevent future cases.

Legionnaires’ Disease Outbreak at a Long-Term Care Facility: Persistence of Low-Level Legionella Contamination in a Water System – North Carolina, 2014: Investigation into a Legionnaires’ outbreak at a long-term care facility shows how difficult it can be to get rid of this deadly bug.

Healthcare-Associated Outbreak of Legionnaires’ Disease on an Inpatient Hematology-Oncology Unit – Alabama, 2014: A hospital unit for immune-compromised cancer patients experienced an outbreak of legionellosis, most likely associated with the hospital’s water system. This outbreak highlights how vulnerable populations are at risk for healthcare-associated infections, including those from environmental exposures.

Surveillance for Large Outbreaks of Tuberculosis -— United States, 2014: Unrecognized contacts of tuberculosis (TB) patients can cause U.S. TB outbreaks to persist for years. Introduction of a novel, national surveillance system using genotyping data to identify new large outbreaks found six probable outbreaks, including three that had not been previously reported to CDC.

Tuberculosis Contact Investigation at an Island Resort – Michigan, 2014: At an island resort in Michigan, a foreign-born seasonal worker came down with tuberculosis -- but the case went undiagnosed for 3 months despite multiple doctor visits. 25 contacts required screening.  Doctor education and increased testing may help.
