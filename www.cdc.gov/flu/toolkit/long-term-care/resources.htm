Available Tools for Increasing Influenza Vaccination among Health Care Personnel in Long-term Settings

Language:
English
  • Español
Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

There are ways to improve health care personnel (HCP) influenza vaccination rates. Helpful resources are organized into the following categories:

Click or tap on a section name to expand each list.

Campaign Materials

Employers
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Seasonal Flu Information for Businesses & Employers. Flyers, posters, and more.
  • Immunization Action Coalition. Declination of Influenza Vaccination Form [65 KB, 1 page]. Fact sheets and other resources.
  • U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. Influenza Vaccination of Health Care Providers in Long-Term Care: Letter from the Assistant Secretary for Health [302 KB, 2 pages]
  • Veterans Affairs Campaign Resources. Website.
Health Care Personnel
  • The Joint Commission. Influenza and Influenza Vaccine Myths and Reality [108 KB, 2 pages]. Fact Sheet.
  • American Medical Directors Association (AMDA). Influenza Immunization and the Health Care Worker (18 minutes). Video.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. I Won’t Spread Flu to My Patients or Family [380 KB, 1 page]. Poster.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Seasonal Influenza Vaccination Resources for Health Professionals. Website.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Continue to Vaccinate Patients and Staff Against the Flu. Podcast.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Influenza Vaccination Information for Health Care Workers. Website.
  • New York Department of Health. For HCP, Protect Your Patients, Protect Your Family, Protect Yourself! [1.5 MB, 1 page]. Poster.
General Public
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. I Can’t Miss Work [1.7 MB, 1 page]. Flyer.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Seasonal Influenza Free Resources. Website.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Everyday Preventive Actions [1.5 MB, 2 pages]. Flyer.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Fight the Flu [200 KB, 1 page]. Flyer.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Take 3 Actions to Fight the Flu [158 KB, 1 page]. Poster.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Take 3 Actions to Fight the Flu [1.9 MB, 2 pages]. Brochure.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. No More Excuses (places to get flu vaccine) [445 KB, 1 page]. Flyer.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Misconceptions about Seasonal Flu and Flu Vaccines. Website.

Toolkits for Establishing and Improving an Influenza Vaccination Program

  • Health Care Facility Toolkit, Health Care Provider Toolkit
    New York State Department of Health. Health Care Facility/ Personnel Influenza Immunization Toolkit. Contains online resources for establishing an influenza vaccination program including information on vaccine documentation, administration (including implementing standing orders), vaccine storage and handling, and educational resources.
  • Toolkit
    Minnesota Department of Health. Long-term Care Influenza. Available online and as a printable resource. Contains information specific to LTC settings.
  • Toolkit [2.3 MB, 73 pages]
    County of San Diego Immunization Branch, Health and Human Services Agency. Immunizations Toolkit for Long-term Care Providers. Available online and as a printable resource. Contains information specific to LTC settings.
  • General Resources
    Immunization Action Coalition. Includes general resources for HCP and their patients regarding influenza illness and the influenza vaccines.
  • Veterans Health Administration’s Seasonal Influenza Manual: Prevention in Action 2013-2014[4.2 MB, 196 pages]
    Provides information about influenza vaccination, education and communication resources, and guidance on how to implement successful influenza prevention campaigns. It is intended for use by the teams and campaign coordinators within VHA facilities.
  • Veterans Health Administration - Seasonal Influenza Campaign Calendar [2.3 MB, 6 pages]
    Provides a timeline and monthly checklists to aid in planning, implementation and evaluation of your facility’s flu vaccination campaign.
  • Checklist of Best Practices for Vaccination Clinics [595 KB, 6 pages], Best Practice Pledge [340 KB, 1 page]
    This checklist is a step-by-step guide to help clinic coordinators/supervisors overseeing vaccination clinics held at satellite, temporary, or off-site locations. This checklist outlines CDC guidelines and best practices that are essential for patient safety and vaccine effectiveness. Questions about the Checklist? Send an email to checklist@izsummitpartners.org. Want to take the pledge? Send an email to vaxclinicpledge@izsummitpartners.org, and someone will be in touch with you.

Cultural and Language Resources

  • HHS National Standards for Culturally and Linguistically Appropriate Services in Health and Health Care
    The National Standards for Culturally and Linguistically Appropriate Services in Health and Health Care (the National CLAS Standards) are intended to advance health equity, improve quality, and help eliminate health care disparities by providing a blueprint for individuals and health and health care organizations to implement culturally and linguistically appropriate services.
  • Influenza Vaccine Information Statements
    Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). Influenza vaccine information statements provided in a number of languages.
  • Seasonal Influenza: Print Materials in Spanish
    CDC. Educational resources on influenza provided in Spanish and organized by target audiences.
  • Declination Form [80 KB, 1 page] in Spanish
    Immunization Action Coalition. Influenza vaccination declination forms in Spanish.
  • The National Influenza Vaccination Disparities Partnership (NIVDP) Resource Kit [48 MB, 35 pages]
    CDC. Information on tailoring messages about influenza and the importance of influenza vaccination across culturally and economically diverse communities.
  • MedlinePlus
    National Library of Medicine. Information about influenza in a number of languages.
  • Public Health–Seattle & King County
    Influenza fact sheets in the following languages: Chinese, Korean, Russian, Spanish, Somali, Vietnamese.
  • Seasonal Flu Materials for Refugees
    Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Printable educational resources written in the native languages of refugee populations commonly resettled in the United States.

Guidance for Promoting Influenza Vaccination in Your Facility

  • Interim Guidance for Influenza Outbreak Management in Long-Term Care Facilities
    CDC. Includes information on influenza surveillance and testing, influenza infection control, treatment, prophylactic use of influenza antivirals, and other resources for managing an influenza outbreak in a long-term care setting.
  • Interventions to Promote Seasonal Influenza Vaccinations among Health Care Workers
    Community Preventive Services Task Force. Recommendations from the Community Preventive Services Task Force. Provides rationale and recommendations based on a systematic review of interventions for promoting influenza vaccination among HCP.
  • Providing a Safer Environment for Health Care Personnel and Patients through Influenza Vaccination. Also available, a one-page summary with page references to the monograph
    The Joint Commission. Informs health care organizations of all types on how to improve seasonal influenza vaccination rates in HCP.
  • Immunizing Health Care Personnel against Influenza: A Report on Best Practices [1.5 MB, 23 pages]
    National Foundation for Infectious Diseases. Presents information as case-studies which can be adapted and modeled by other organizations.
  • Recommendations on Strategies to Achieve the Healthy People 2020 Annual Influenza Vaccine Coverage Goal for Health Care Personnel [252 KB, 44 pages]
    National Vaccine Advisory Committee. Presents a set of tiered strategies to help build an influenza vaccination program in facilities towards the goal of annually reaching 90% coverage, the Healthy People 2020 influenza vaccine coverage goal for HCP. Also includes a discussion of the ethical and legal considerations of implementing mandatory policies in health care settings.
  • National Action Plan to Prevent Health Care Associated Infections: Roadmap to Elimination. Influenza Vaccination of Health Care Personnel [427 KB, 20 pages]
    U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. Provides a link to a section of the HHS National Action Plan to Prevent Health Care Associated Infections: Road Map to Elimination (HAI Action Plan) describing influenza vaccination of HCP as an important strategy to reducing health care associated influenza outbreaks in health care settings.
