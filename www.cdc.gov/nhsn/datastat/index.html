  • Summary Report for Blood and Body Fluid Exposure Data Collected from Participating Healthcare Facilities (June 1995 through December 2007) [PDF - 3 MB]

    This report describes to the public health, the occupational health and safety, and infection control communities, the variety of occupational exposures to blood and body fluids that occur among healthcare providers (HCP).

    The National Surveillance System for Healthcare Workers (NaSH) was a voluntary surveillance system developed by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to systematically collect information important to the prevention of occupational exposures and infections among healthcare personnel (HCP). NaSH was established in 1995 by the Hospital Infections Program, National Center for Infectious Disease, in consultation with other divisions and institutes within CDC. NaSH collected surveillance data through 2007, and became a legacy system to the web-based National Healthcare Safety Network (NHSN) Healthcare Personnel Safety Component which was launched in August 2009.
