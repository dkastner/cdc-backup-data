National Tobacco Control Program Funding

Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

CDC is the only federal agency that provides funding to help support all 50 states, the District of Columbia, 8 U.S. territories, and 12 tribal organizations for comprehensive tobacco control efforts. Tobacco prevention and control is one of the “best buys” in public health: states with strong tobacco control programs have demonstrated achievement of a $55:$1 return1 on their investment, mostly in averted health care costs to treat smoking-related illness.

Examples of Recent Successes

The program has achieved many successes.

  • Maryland launched an initiative to target illegal tobacco sales to youth. Through retailer education and partnerships between the state and retailers, illegal sales to youth declined by 56%.
  • Missouri is partnering with colleges and universities to establish smoke-free and tobacco free campuses. Missouri increased the number of colleges and universities with smoke-free campuses to 32, of which five have tobacco-free status, prohibiting use of all forms of tobacco use on campus.
  • Oklahoma is working with health systems to increase quitline use among the Medicaid population. The state has removed barriers to utilization, and no longer requires preauthorization or copays for medication. Oklahoma is also coordinating with county health departments so that clients who come in for other health department services are referred to the quitline when appropriate.
  • Kentucky participated in a pilot during the Tips advertising campaign to promote free nicotine replacement therapy (NRT) to callers of the state quitline. During the two-week promotion, call volume increased by 150% and NRT utilization increased by over 800%. After the pilot, the state continued to provide NRT for an additional 8 weeks and continued to experience high call volume.
  • Washington has developed a smart phone application that is being utilized by young adult smokers (age 18-34). The smart phone app has a quit rate comparable to the state’s quitline and can be an innovative way to reach smokers who prefer a more private and more tech-based approach to quitting.

2016 Funding

In FY 2016, CDC supported comprehensive tobacco control programs in all 50 states, the District of Columbia, 8 US territories or jurisdictions, and 12 tribal organizations. In addition, CDC provided funding to states and territories to ensure and expand state quitline capacity.
