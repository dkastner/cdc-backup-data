AFIX Quarterly Conference Call Minutes

April 15, 2015: Content on this page kept for historical reasons.

March 26, 2015

Minutes

Announcements

  • The focus of today’s call (March 26) is to provide an overview on conducting an AFIX feedback and follow-up with providers. The content is intended to serve as training for new AFIX staff, and a reminder for AFIX coordinators and reviewers.
  • The next AFIX Quarterly Call is scheduled for May 13, 2015. Any unplanned changes to scheduled calls will be communicated to awardees a couple of weeks in advance. The agenda for the May 13th call will include an analysis and discussion of AFIXAR 2014 data.

New Documents

To better assist AFIX awardees, we developed several supplementary documents. The use of the documents is not a requirement. They are intended to serve as beneficial tools for additional support during a visit.

AFIX Site Visit Checklist
  • A comprehensive checklist composed of a pre-visit to do list, a list of documents to bring on a site visit and what to leave behind with the provider.
  • This document was designed to better assist the AFIX reviewer in preparing for a site visit.
AFIX Program Overview
  • A comprehensive guide on AFIX, which includes the program overview, mission, and how to conduct an AFIX assessment, feedback and follow-up session.
  • This guide was designed to familiarize providers with your AFIX program. We encourage you to fill out the AFIX Program Overview with AFIX examples relevant to your jurisdiction for the document to properly reflect your immunization program.
  • We believe the Program Overview will be beneficial for AFIX reviewers as a quick reminder of key points to be discussed during an AFIX session, as well as a detailed guide for the provider practice.
AFIX Quality Improvement (QI) Action Plan
  • This document describes how to create a Quality Improvement (QI) Action Plan and includes an easy to use template.
  • You are welcome to add additional fields to the template to better suit your immunization program, or simply complete this form and use it as your QI Action Plan.
eXchange of Information Plan
  • This document is an overview on conducting a follow-up visit. It provides a detailed guide on conducting the initial and subsequent follow-up, as well as information on educational and phone sessions.
  • The appendix provides a list of possible barriers to implementation and a sample letter to the provider in preparation for a subsequent visit.

Assessment

Quantitative
  • The quantitative portion of an AFIX assessment consists of generating assessment reports for children and/or adolescents for the provider practice. The reports can be generated through IIS (Immunization Information Systems) or CoCASA (Comprehensive Clinic Assessment Software Application).

  • CoCASA

    • At the provider office, the AFIX reviewer will select a sample of charts and enter the data manually into CoCASA. Using this tool, rates are based on the sample size assessed by the reviewer.

  • IIS

    • When using IIS, assessment coverage reports are generated from the registry. This method allows for the reviewer to assess the entire population of children and/or adolescents within a specific age range at the provider site.
Qualitative
  • The AFIX Site Visit Questionnaire is the primary tool used for the qualitative portion of an AFIX assessment. This is a requirement for every AFIX visit.
  • The purpose of the Site Visit Questionnaire is to assess the provider’s current immunization practices and engage with the provider staff to develop strategies for increasing vaccination uptake among their patient population.
  • The questionnaire is designed to assess the root cause of missed opportunities and provide guidance in developing sustainable change.

Feedback

  • Feedback is the process of informing immunization providers and staff about observations and results from the Site Visit Questionnaire. Feedback provides a forum to discuss the provider’s immunization delivery system, missed opportunities, and steps to improve quality and coverage.
  • During the feedback session, the AFIX coordinator will review and discuss the assessment reports, completed AFIX Site Visit Questionnaire, and assist in choosing 2-3 quality improvement strategies. These strategies should be chosen based on responses of “No” in the questionnaire, then added to the QI Action Plan.
  • Creating a QI Action Plan is a CDC requirement during an AFIX visit. You are welcome to create your own or use the CDC QI Action Plan Template. (This document is attached with the meeting minutes and can also be found on AFIX Online in the “Reports” tab).

eXchange (Follow-Up)

  • The eXchange (follow-up) portion of the AFIX visit is conducted 3-6 months after the initial site visit. The purpose of the follow-up visit is to assess progress on the QI strategies and changes in the clinics coverage rates.

  • Please note: The eXchange is a CDC requirement. Thus follow-up must be conducted with each AFIX visit in order for the visit to be complete.

    • Follow-up can be conducted in person, via phone, email, fax or webinar. Subsequent follow-up is conducted after the initial eXchange if the reviewer believed the clinic would benefit from additional assistance in improving coverage rates and implementing QI strategies.
  • For additional information on the eXchange portion of AFIX, please see the eXchange of Information Plan. (This document is attached with the meeting minutes and can also be found on AFIX Online in the “Reports” tab.)

Tips on Communicating with Providers

  • A critical component of a successful AFIX session is securing buy-in from the lead physician at the practice. Please see below a list of tips on how to effectively engage the provider and encourage him/her to implement the necessary changes for improving the clinic’s immunization program.
    • Communicate that you will only need 15 minutes.
    • Present a provider report card comparing the clinic’s rates with other practices in the county and the state average.
    • Provide positive feedback on their work along with recommendations for improvement.
    • Leave behind a cover with bullet points on key topics covered during your discussion.
    • Send an overview email a week later reminding the physician of your conversation and asking if there is any additional support they can provide at this point.
  • For additional assistance, please review the document entitled “Tips for Communicating with Physicians Before, During and After AFIX Visits.”
