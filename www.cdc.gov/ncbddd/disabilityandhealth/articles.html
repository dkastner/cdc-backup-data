Key Findings: Adults with One or More Functional Disabilities
The Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report (MMWR) has published a new report that describes the demographic and socioeconomic characteristics of working-age (18-64 years) adults with disabilities living in U.S. communities.

Key Findings: Socioeconomic Factors at the Intersection of Race and Ethnicity Influencing Health Risks for People with Disabilities
The Journal of Racial and Ethnic Health Disparities has published a new study looking at the relationship between income and education, and two health behaviors—smoking and obesity—among people with disabilities from different racial and ethnic groups.

Key Findings: Including People with Disabilities in Community Health Initiatives
The BMC Public Health Journal recently published a study about the Community Health Inclusion Index (CHII), a new evaluation tool for improving inclusion of people with disabilities in community health initiatives.

Key Findings: Disability within US Public Health School and Program Curricula
The Massachusetts Department of Public Health in collaboration with the CDC has published an article that indicates that only half of U.S public health schools and programs offer courses with disability content. A public health workforce with the knowledge and skills to work with people with disabilities can help make sure that everybody have access to services and programs they need to live healthy and productive lives. Schools should plan to include disability content in their curricula.

Key Findings: Prevalence of Disability and Disability Type among Adults, United States – 2013
The Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report (MMWR) has published a new report describing the percentage of adults with disabilities in the United States living in communities.

Key Findings: Tools for Improving Clinical Preventive Services Receipt Among Women with Disabilities of Childbearing Ages and Beyond
The Maternal and Child Health Journal published findings of a search for tools to improve use of clinical preventive services among women with disabilities.

Key Findings: Persons with Disabilities as an Unrecognized Health Disparity Population
The American Journal of Public Health published a commentary in its Framing Health Matters section focused on the importance of addressing health disparities among people with disabilities in relation to access to health care, health behaviors, health status, and social factors that impact health.

Key Findings: Disability Status, Mortality, and Leading Causes of Death in the United States Community Population
CDC researchers have published an article in the journal Medical Care that examines the relationship between disability and death using data from a nationally representative survey of U.S. adults, ages 18 and older, living in communities.

Key Findings:  Hypertension Among US Adults by Disability Status and Type, National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey, 2001–2010
The journal Preventing Chronic Disease published a new study “Hypertension Among US Adults by Disability Status and Type, National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey, 2001-2010.”  

Helping Everyone Quit Smoking
If more people with disabilities are included in smoking cessation programs, the percentage of those who smoke can be reduced.
(Published: November 22, 2016)

Disability Inclusion Works for All
Building an inclusive workforce where people with disabilities can actively participate and contribute is everybody’s job.
(Published: October 3, 2016)

26th ADA Anniversary: Health Is for All of Us
Adults with disabilities experience significant differences in health compared to adults who do not have disabilities. Learn how the Disability and Health Data System (DHDS) can provide information about the characteristics and needs of adults with disabilities in your state.
(Published: July 23, 2016)

Physical Activity is for Everybody
Learn how people with disabilities can include physical activity in their lives.
(Published: May 23, 2016)

International Day of Persons with Disabilities
Learn about walkable and accessible communities that can improve the health of people with disabilities.
(Published: December 9, 2015)

Emergency Readiness for People with Disabilities
Learn how people with disabilities, their families, and first responders can plan ahead for a disaster.
(Published: September 28, 2015)

Behind the Scenes at the Special Olympics World Games 2015
Healthy Athletes program during Special Olympics sporting events have led to more than 1.6 million health examinations of people with intellectual disabilities.
(Published: July 23, 2015)

Limb Loss Awareness Month
April is Limb Loss Awareness Month: an excellent opportunity to raise awareness about the limb loss community
(Published: April 20, 2015)

American Heart Month
February is American Heart Month: Learn how to keep your blood pressure under control
(Published: February 9, 2015)

New Features added to Disability and Health Data System (DHDS)
There are 1 in 5 adults in the U.S. living with a disability. To reduce these health disparities, state-based data are needed to provide support for programs and services that can effectively improve the health of people with disabilities.
(Published: June 24, 2013)
