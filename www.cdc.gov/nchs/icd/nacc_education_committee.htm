WHO FIC Education and Implementation Committee

Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

On this Page

  • What's New
  • Meetings
  • International ICD-10 Training Materials Products and Training Capacity

The WHO FIC Education and Implementation Committee was created at the 2010 WHO FIC Network Annual meeting in Toronto, Canada, when the separate Education Committee and Implementation Committee merged. These two committees had worked closely together since their establishment in 1999, first focusing on the International Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems (ICD) and, beginning in 2003, on the International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health, as well.

Training and Certification Program for International Classification of Diseases (ICD)

  • Evaluation of the International Training and Certification Program for International Classification of Diseases (ICD) [PDF - 240 KB]
    This report describes a pilot study to evaluate the International Training and Certification Program for ICD-10 Coders, with a focus on the assessment and recognition of underlying cause-of-death coders and trainers. The International Program is a joint collaboration established in 2004 between the WHO-FIC Education Committee and the International Federation of Health Records Organizations in 2004.
  • Core Curricula for Training ICD-10 Coders [PDF - 68 KB]
    The core curricula for Mortality and Morbidity Coders were developed by the WHO FIC Education Committee and endorsed by the WHO FIC Network and the International Federation of Health Records Organization
  • Core Curriculum for Certifiers of Underlying Cause of Death [PDF - 40 KB]
    The Core Curriculum and Best Practices for Certifiers of Underlying Cause of Death were developed by the WHO-FIC Education Committee in 2007.

ICF Training

Curriculum Modules for ICF, Version 1 [PDF - 54 KB]
The Curriculum Modules provide a basis for education on the International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF) and its derived classification for children and youth (ICF-CY) for potential users and in all countries. They were developed by the WHO-FIC Education Committee and Functioning and Disability Reference Group and endorsed by the WHO-FIC Network Council in 2008.
