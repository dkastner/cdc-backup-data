Quarterly Age-specific Birth Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • Birth rates for the second quarter of 2016 declined for the following maternal age groups in comparison with the second quarter of the previous year: 15–19 (from 21.4 births per 1,000 women to 19.3), 20–24 (from 74.4 to 70.8), and 25–29 (from 103.3 to 101.2).
  • In contrast, birth rates were higher in the second quarter of 2016 compared to the second quarter of 2015 for women aged 30–34 (from 101.8 to 102.7), 35-39 (from 51.0 to 51.8), and 40–44 (from 10.5 to 10.8).
  • No change was seen for women aged 45 and over.

12 Month-ending Age-specific Birth Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • Birth rates for the 12-month period ending with the second quarter of 2016 declined for the following maternal age groups in comparison with the previous year ending with the second quarter of 2015: 15–19 (from 23.4 to 21.2), 20–24 (from 78.3 to 75.3), and 25–29 (from 105.4 to 103.2).
  • In contrast, 12 month-ending birth rates were higher in the year ending with the second quarter of 2016 compared with the year ending with the second quarter of 2015 for maternal age groups 30–34 (from 101.1 to 102.3), 35–39 (from 51.4 to 52.1), 40–44 (from 10.8 to 11.2).
  • No change was seen for women aged 45 and over.

Quarterly General Fertility Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The general fertility rate decreased from 61.6 births per 1,000 women aged 15-44 in the second quarter of 2015 to 60.9 in the second quarter of 2016.

12 Month-ending General Fertility Rate: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The 12 month-ending general fertility rate declined from 62.9 in the year ending with the second quarter of 2015 to 62.3 in the year ending with the second quarter of 2016.

Quarterly Teen Birth Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The teen birth rate declined from 21.4 births per 1,000 females aged 15–19 in the second quarter of 2015 to 19.3 in the second quarter of 2016.
  • Birth rates for females aged 15–17 declined from 9.4 in the second quarter of 2015 to 8.3 in the second quarter of 2016.
  • Birth rates for females aged 18–19 declined from 39.3 in the second quarter of 2015 to 35.7 in the second quarter of 2016.
  • Birth rates for females aged 10–14 declined from 0.3 in the second quarter of 2015 to 0.2 in the second quarter of 2016.

12 Month-ending Teen Birth Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • Birth rates for the 12-month period ending with the second quarter of 2016 declined in comparison with the previous year ending with the second quarter of 2015 for teenagers 15–19 years old (from 23.4 per 1,000 to 21.2).
  • Birth rates declined over this time period for the following maternal age groups: 10–14 (from 0.3 to 0.2), 15–17 (from 10.5 to 9.3), and 18–19 (from 42.4 to 39.1).

Quarterly Total and Low-risk Cesarean Delivery Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • There was no change in the percentage of cesarean deliveries from the second quarter of 2015 to the second quarter of 2016 (31.9%).
  • The percentage of low-risk cesarean deliveries remained the same from the second quarter of 2015 to the second quarter of 2016 (25.8%).

12 Month-ending Total and Low-risk Cesarean Delivery Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The 12 month-ending percentage of cesarean deliveries decreased from 32.1% in the second quarter of 2015 to 32.0% in the second quarter of 2016.
  • The 12 month-ending percentage of low-risk cesarean deliveries remained the same from the year ending with the second quarter of 2015 to the year ending with the second quarter of 2016 (25.8%).

Quarterly Preterm Birth Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The preterm birth rate (less than 37 weeks) increased from 9.68% of births in the second quarter of 2015 to 9.97% in the second quarter of 2016.
  • Within this group, the percentage of infants born at 34-36 weeks (late preterm) increased from 6.84% to 7.14% while no difference was seen for infants born at less than 34 weeks gestation (early preterm).

12 Month-ending Preterm Birth Rates: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The 12 month-ending preterm birth rate (less than 37 weeks) increased from 9.54% of births for the second quarter of 2015 to 9.76% of births for the second quarter of 2016.
  • The 12 month-ending percentage of infants born between 34-36 weeks increased from 6.79% for the second quarter of 2015 to 7.00% for the second quarter of 2016, while no change was seen in the percentage of infants born early preterm (less than 34 weeks).

Quarterly Birth Rates at Term: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The percentage of infants born early term (37-38 weeks gestation) was higher in the second quarter of 2016 (25.34%) compared with the second quarter of 2015 (24.83%).
  • The percentage of infants born full term (39-40 weeks gestation) and late term (41 weeks) declined in 2016 Q1 compared with 2015 Q1; from 58.54 % to 57.90% for full term, and from 6.54% to 6.44% for late term.

12 Month-ending Birth Rates at Term: United States, 2014–Quarter 2, 2016

  • The 12 month-ending percentage of infants born early term (37-38 weeks gestation) was higher in the second quarter of 2016 (25.22%) compared with the second quarter of 2015 (24.85%).
  • The 12 month-ending percentage of infants born full term (39-40 weeks gestation) was lower in the second quarter of 2016 (58.20%) compared with the second quarter of 2015 (58.63%).
  • The 12 month-ending percentage of infants born late term (41 weeks gestation) was lower in the second quarter of 2016 (6.46%) compared with the second quarter of 2015 (6.56%).
