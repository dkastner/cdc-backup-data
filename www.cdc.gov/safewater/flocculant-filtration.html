A P&Gâ¢ sachet (Proctor & Gamble)

The Procter & Gamble Company developed P&G Purifier of Waterâ¢ in conjunction with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). P&Gâ¢ sachets are now centrally produced in Pakistan, and sold to non-governmental organizations (NGOs) worldwide at a cost of 3.5 US cents per sachet. The P&Gâ¢ product is a small sachet containing powdered ferric sulfate (a flocculant) and calcium hypochlorite (a disinfectant). P&Gâ¢ was designed to reverse-engineer a water treatment plant, incorporating the multiple barrier processes of removal of particles and disinfection. To treat water with P&Gâ¢, users open the sachet, add the contents to an open bucket containing 10 liters of water, stir for 5 minutes, let the solids settle to the bottom of the bucket, strain the water through a cotton cloth into a second container, and wait 20 minutes for the hypochlorite to inactivate the microorganisms.

Lab Effectiveness, Field Effectiveness, and Health Impact

The flocculant/disinfectant powder P&Gâ¢ has been proven to remove the vast majority of bacteria, viruses, and protozoa, even in highly turbid waters. P&Gâ¢ has also been documented to reduce diarrheal disease from 90% to less than 16% incidence in five randomized, controlled health intervention studies. P&Gâ¢ also removes heavy metalsâsuch as arsenicâand chemical contaminantsâsuch as pesticidesâfrom water. Studies showing the efficacy of P&Gâ¢ have been conducted for highly turbid water in the laboratory, in developing countries, in rural and urban areas, refugee camps, and include all age groups.

Benefits, Drawbacks, and Appropriateness

The benefits of Flocculant/Disinfectant Powder are:

  • Proven reduction of bacteria, viruses, and protozoa in water
  • Removal of heavy metals and chemicals
  • Increased free chlorine protection against contamination
  • Proven reduction of diarrheal disease
  • Visual improvement of water and acceptability
  • Transport of sachets easy
  • Long shelf life of sachets

The drawbacks of Flocculant/Disinfectant Powder are:

  • Multiple steps are necessaryârequires training or demonstration
  • Requires a lot of equipment (2 buckets, cloth, and a stirrer)
  • The higher relative cost per liter of water treated

P&Gâ¢ is most appropriate in areas with a consistent supply chain for sachet resupply and in urban, rural, and emergency situations when educational messages can reach users to encourage correct and consistent use.

Implementation Examples

Social marketing organizations, such as the NGO Population Services International, sell P&Gâ¢ sachets in multiple countries.

Local organizations use the socially marketed P&Gâ¢ sachets in their own programming to provide safe drinking water. In western Kenya, students in schools are taught how and why to use P&Gâ¢, and safe water clubs treat drinking water for all the students. Also in Kenya, HIV self-help groups sell P&Gâ¢ sachets and storage containers as an income-generating activity.

P&Gâ¢ sachets have been widely used to respond to emergencies â from the 2004 tsunami in Indonesia to flooding in Haiti to cholera epidemics in Africa. Economics and Scalability

Each sachet of P&Gâ¢ is provided to global emergency relief organizations or non-governmental organizations at a cost of 3.5 US cents, not including shipping from Pakistan by ocean container. Transport, distribution, education, and community motivation can add significantly to program costs. Sachets are generally sold at product cost recovery for 10 US cents each, for a cost of 1 US cent per liter treated. Currently, P&Gâ¢ projects operate either on partial cost recovery (charging the user only for the product, and subsidizing program costs with donor funds), or fully subsidized free distribution such as in emergency situations. Procter & Gamble sells the P&Gâ¢ sachets at cost, makes no profits on P&Gâ¢ sales, and donates programmatic funding to some projects.
