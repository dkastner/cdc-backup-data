Division of Health Informatics and Surveillance (DHIS)

Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

DHIS provides leadership and crosscutting support in

  • developing public health information systems,

  • managing public health surveillance programs, and

  • providing health-related data required to monitor, control, and prevent the occurrence and spread of diseases and other adverse health conditions.

  • NNDSS Modernization Initiative (NMI) Modernizing the systems and processes used to receive nationally notifiable disease data

  • National Syndromic Surveillance Program (NSSP) Improving CDC’s activities in public health surveillance

  • CDC WONDER Health-related data sets to aid information-driven decision making for public health

  • Surveillance Resource Center Surveillance resources at your fingertips

  • Epi InfoTM Essential free software for public health practice

  • CDC Surveillance Strategy Improving the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention's activities in public health surveillance

  • NNDSS Monitoring, controlling, and preventing notifiable diseases and conditions

DHIS Newsroom & Resource Center

Current Issue of NMI Notes Highlights February NMI eSHARE Focused on NMI Implementation Preparation 101

The February issue of NMI Notes  highlights that the February NMI eSHARE webinar, “Implementation Preparation 101,” will provide detailed information on how jurisdictions can best prepare for implementing the Generic v2 and Hepatitis case notification messages. The event will be held on Tuesday, 2/21/17, at 3:00 to 4:00 PM.

Mission and Vision

DHIS Mission:
DHIS provides leadership and crosscutting support in

  • developing public health information systems,
  • managing public health surveillance programs, and
  • providing health-related data required to monitor, control, and prevent the occurrence and spread of diseases and other adverse health conditions.

DHIS Vision:
Health decisions and actions are guided by timely and useful information.
