2015 Updates from the Division of Global Health Protection

  • December 2015: Global Disease Detection: A Commitment to Global Health Security
    Learn more about the innovation, determination, and resourcefulness it takes to protect people all over the world through advancing the science of global public health. (PDF)
  • December 2015: Global Disease Detection: Division of Global Health Protection Partner Announcement
    Learn about the Global Disease Detection program from SARS to Ebola and the future
  • November 2015: Stop the Global Threat of Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)
    Support WHO World Antibiotics Week: November 16-22, 2015
  • July 2015: Tobacco, Sexual Violence, NCDs, & More
    Learn more about noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) and CDC’s commitment to working with partners to address this global priority. (PDF)
  • July 2015: CDC Releases Coverage of International Health Regulations (IHR)
    CDC has devoted a series of articles and infographics to helping you understand how the IHR are connected to global health security, as well as the impact these important regulations have on the work we’re doing to keep people safe around the world.
  • April 2015: Global Health Security
    The Ebola epidemic is an unprecedented event in our history and, while it has presented many challenges, it has also given rise to opportunities for the public and private sectors to work together toward a common goal of building capacity to prevent, detect, and rapidly respond to disease threats before they become epidemics. (PDF)
  • February 2015: Hypertension Prevention, Treatment, and Control: Successful Global Strategies
    Join CDC’s Director Dr. Thomas Frieden and leading hypertension experts in discussing practical ways to successfully control hypertension and decrease heart disease and stroke in the U.S. and around the world.
